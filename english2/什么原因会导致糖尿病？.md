---
大多数糖人从正常人变成糖人是其长期虐待自己的胰岛系统的结果。这个虐待模式就是饮食无度+久坐不动。饮食无度，摄入食物的总量就多，尤其是摄入的碳水多多，而久坐不动使身体的耗糖少，体内糖多多(骨骼肌糖原满满，肝糖原满满，血糖满满)。
For most diabetics, the shift from being healthy to having diabetes is the result of long-term abuse of their pancreatic islet system. This abusive pattern consists of **excessive eating plus a sedentary lifestyle**. Overeating leads to excessive total food intake—especially high carbohydrate consumption—while a sedentary routine reduces the body’s sugar consumption, leaving the body overloaded with sugar (skeletal muscle glycogen full, liver glycogen full, blood sugar full).

---
在身体满糖的条件下，继续高碳水油脂摄入，血糖高升，迫使胰岛素大量分泌，而降糖效果不彰，结果进一步迫使胰岛素分泌，使胰岛β细胞处于过劳状态。
When the body is already saturated with sugar, continued intake of high-carbohydrate and high-fat foods causes blood sugar to surge. This forces the pancreas to secrete large amounts of insulin, but the blood sugar-lowering effect remains poor. As a result, insulin secretion is further stimulated, pushing pancreatic β-cells into a state of overwork.

---
长期过劳，造成不可逆的器质性病变，出现各种程度的胰岛素失效(出工不出力了)，这就是胰岛素抵抗。
Prolonged overwork leads to irreversible organic damage, resulting in varying degrees of insulin ineffectiveness (working without delivering results)—this is **insulin resistance**.

---
胰岛素抵抗使得大量的胰岛素分泌也不能抑制住餐后血糖的飙升，使人长期处于高血糖状态，这就是糖人了。同时，还使人长期处于高胰岛素血症状态，这就是胰人了。
Insulin resistance means that even massive insulin secretion cannot suppress postprandial blood sugar spikes, trapping the body in a chronic state of hyperglycemia—and that is what makes someone a diabetic. At the same time, it also causes persistent hyperinsulinemia, turning the person into what we might call an “insulin-overloaded patient”.

---
糖人的器官长期浸泡在高血糖环境中，被糖化的机会大量增加，之后成为糖肾人，糖眼人，糖腿人，糖血管人…各色疾病患者。
The organs of diabetics are constantly bathed in a high-sugar environment, greatly increasing the chance of **glycation**. Eventually, this leads to complications such as diabetic nephropathy, diabetic retinopathy, diabetic foot, and diabetic angiopathy—turning patients into sufferers of various diabetes-related diseases.

---
胰人长期处于高胰岛素血症状态，高胰岛素会干扰各类细胞的正常工作，引发疾病。比如，某种高血压就是高胰岛素血症导致的。
People with chronic hyperinsulinemia have high insulin levels interfering with the normal function of various cells, triggering illnesses. For instance, certain types of hypertension are caused by hyperinsulinemia.

---
我们看到听到的多是高血糖血症带来的恶果，很少看到听到高胰岛素血症带来的恶果，其原因或是二者是成对出现的，归因到一个上面也没大错。
Most of the adverse effects we hear about are attributed to hyperglycemia, while those caused by hyperinsulinemia are rarely mentioned. One reason may be that these two conditions occur together, so attributing all consequences to one is not a major mistake.

---
另外一个是检测胰岛素不方便，糖人不能及时察觉到高胰岛素血症，就不太谈它了。但高胰岛素血症的危害或更大，更隐蔽。
Another reason is that insulin testing is inconvenient; diabetics cannot detect hyperinsulinemia in a timely manner, so the condition is rarely discussed. However, the harm caused by hyperinsulinemia may be greater and more insidious.

---
只关注血糖高，忽略胰岛素高，就会出歪招，比如为控糖而主张“少食多餐”。
Focusing solely on high blood sugar while ignoring high insulin levels leads to ill-advised strategies, such as advocating “eating small, frequent meals” for blood sugar control.

---
这样，单看血糖，好像每餐都比较少的碳水摄入，餐后血糖就容易控制住，但其实这回多次引发高胰岛素状态，是胰岛素长时间处于高水平，危害身体更甚。
On the surface, this approach seems effective: lower carbohydrate intake per meal makes postprandial blood sugar easier to control. But in reality, it repeatedly triggers high insulin levels, keeping insulin elevated for extended periods—which is even more harmful to the body.

---
成糖人是正常人自己害自己的典型过程。糖人成糖肾人则有可能是一个无奈的过程，虐待胰岛系统太久太甚，过劳损伤太重，之后再挽回，虽可延缓成糖肾人的进度，但势不可挡了。
Becoming a diabetic is a classic example of self-inflicted harm. For a diabetic to develop diabetic nephropathy, however, it may be an inevitable process. When the pancreatic islet system has been abused for too long and sustained severe overwork damage, subsequent interventions can only slow down the progression of nephropathy, but cannot stop it.

---
正常人要不成糖人是事在人为的事，但想糖人不成糖肾人，除了拼命自救外，更要拜托老天恩赐了。
Avoiding diabetes as a healthy person is achievable through personal effort. But for a diabetic to avoid diabetic nephropathy, in addition to desperate self-help measures, they are mostly at the mercy of fate.

---
与其在糖人阶段辛苦努力还要听天由命，不如在正常人时就做好饮食管理+运动管理，使自己永不成糖人。
Instead of struggling and leaving things to chance after developing diabetes, it is better to practice strict diet and exercise management while healthy to ensure you never become diabetic in the first place.
