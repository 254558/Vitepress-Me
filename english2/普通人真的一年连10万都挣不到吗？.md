# 普通人真的一年连10万都挣不到吗？
普通人真的一年连10万都挣不到吗？
Can an ordinary person really not earn 100,000 yuan a year?

---
我朋友在东莞开了18年模具厂，他给我看了看厂里2025年全年的工资流水，厂里最顶尖的技术工，每天干11个小时，一个月只休2天，算上所有加班费，一年的税前收入是9.2万。
My friend has run a mold factory in Dongguan for 18 years. He showed me the 2025 annual salary records of his factory. The top technician there works 11 hours a day and only takes 2 days off a month. Even with all overtime pay included, his pre-tax annual income is only 92,000 yuan.

---
连这个全厂“兵王”都没摸到10万的边。
Not even this "top gun" of the factory can get close to the 100,000-yuan threshold.

---
这还是技术工。
And this is for a skilled worker, mind you.

---
普通人要挣够10万，意味着月薪必须达到8333元。
For an ordinary person to earn 100,000 yuan a year, it means their monthly salary has to hit 8,333 yuan.

---
现在的行情是，北京、上海、深圳的保安，月薪在5500到6500元之间，这已经是管住的情况。如果不管住你看看。
As things stand now, security guards in Beijing, Shanghai and Shenzhen earn between 5,500 and 6,500 yuan a month—and that’s with accommodation provided. Just imagine how low it would be without housing included.

---
一个保安要挣到8333元，他需要每天站岗16个小时，或者同时打两份工，人的心脏和脊椎撑不住这种强度的物理磨损。
To make 8,333 yuan a month, a security guard would have to stand duty 16 hours a day, or work two jobs at the same time. No human heart or spine can withstand such physical wear and tear.

---
这不是努不努力的问题，是命只有一条。
This isn’t a matter of working hard or not—it’s that we only get one life.

---
就刚刚刷到的，某二线省会城市2025年的岗位公开招聘信息，该市55%的岗位起薪在3500到4500元。
I just saw a public recruitment notice for 2025 in a second-tier provincial capital city—55% of the positions there offer a starting salary between 3,500 and 4,500 yuan.

---
如果要从4500元涨到8333元，你需要在这个公司连续升职3次，或者跳槽5次且每次涨薪30%。
To jump from 4,500 yuan to 8,333 yuan a month, you’d need to get three consecutive promotions at the same company, or switch jobs five times with a 30% pay raise each time.

---
在现在的环境下，这叫白日做梦。
In the current job market, that’s nothing short of a pipe dream.

---
中国14亿人里，有将近9亿人没有本科学历，这9亿人的就业范围被锁死在了餐厅、工厂、建筑工地和快递路上。
Of China’s 1.4 billion people, nearly 900 million don’t have a bachelor’s degree. Their employment options are confined to restaurants, factories, construction sites and delivery routes.

---
这些行业的单价是由什么决定的？是由极度过剩的劳动力供给决定的。
What determines the pay rate in these industries? It’s determined by the extreme oversupply of labor.

---
你去应聘一个送外卖的岗，你不干，后面有10个人排队等着干，这就导致了单价会一直被压到生存线的边缘。
If you apply for a food delivery job and turn it down, there are 10 more people waiting in line for it. This drives the per-order pay down to the brink of a subsistence wage.

---
现在在很多城市送外卖已经降到了3.5元一单。
Nowadays, in many cities, delivery riders only get 3.5 yuan per order.

---
如果你想一年挣10万，去掉每月的电瓶车租赁费、话费和修车费，你每天需要送够85单。
If you want to earn 100,000 yuan a year, after deducting monthly e-bike rental fees, phone bills and maintenance costs, you’d have to complete 85 orders every single day.

---
一单20分钟，85单需要1700分钟。
At 20 minutes per order, 85 orders would take 1,700 minutes.

---
一天只有1440分钟。
But there are only 1,440 minutes in a day.

---
你就算不睡觉、不吃饭、不上厕所，你也跑不出来这10万块钱。
Even if you never slept, ate or used the bathroom, you still couldn’t make that 100,000 yuan.

---
再说说那些坐在办公室里的“普通人”。
Let’s talk about those "ordinary people" who work in offices.

---
我认识一个武汉的姑娘，29岁，在一家传媒公司做策划，她的月薪是6000元，她跟我算过一笔账。
I know a 29-year-old girl in Wuhan who works as a planner at a media company, earning 6,000 yuan a month. She once crunched the numbers for me.

---
她每天早上一顿热干面加蛋酒，8块，中午点个最便宜的外卖，25块，晚上回家自己煮个面，加个蛋，5块。
Every morning, she has a bowl of hot dry noodles with egg wine for 8 yuan; at noon, she orders the cheapest takeout for 25 yuan; in the evening, she cooks herself a bowl of noodles with an egg for 5 yuan.

---
一天的伙食费是38块，一个月1140块。
That’s 38 yuan a day for food, or 1,140 yuan a month.

---
房租1800块，通勤地铁费240块，手机费100块。
Rent is 1,800 yuan, commuting by subway costs 240 yuan, and her phone bill is 100 yuan.

---
这些是维持她作为一个“城市人”活着的底线支出，一共3280块。
These are the basic expenses to keep her afloat as a "city dweller"—totaling 3,280 yuan.

---
她每个月只能剩下2720块。
She only has 2,720 yuan left every month.

---
普通人挣不到10万，是因为系统在抽水。
Ordinary people can’t earn 100,000 yuan a year because the system is siphoning away their income.

---
房租是抽水机，医疗是抽水机，教育是抽水机。
Rent is a siphon, healthcare is a siphon, education is a siphon.

---
今天在有一个帖子讨论得很火，叫《消失的年终奖》。
There’s a trending post today called *The Vanishing Year-End Bonus*.

---
以前很多人靠年终奖冲刺10万年薪，但2025年到2026年的跨年季，大多数中小企业的年终奖变成了两箱苹果或者一张超市卡。
Once, many people relied on year-end bonuses to hit the 100,000-yuan annual income mark. But during the 2025-2026 New Year period, most small and medium-sized enterprises replaced bonuses with two boxes of apples or a supermarket gift card.

---
甚至有的公司直接宣布，因为经营困难，取消第13个月工资，这直接把很多原本站在9万边缘的人，一脚踢回了7万。
Some companies even announced they were scrapping the 13th-month salary due to operational difficulties—this instantly knocked many people who were hovering around 90,000 yuan a year back down to 70,000 yuan.

---
那为什么网络上看起来人均百万？
So why does it seem like everyone online earns a million yuan a year?

---
因为声音是有阶级的。
Because voice has a class divide.

---
一个年入20万的白领，他有心情在小红书上发精致的下午茶，他有话语权。
A white-collar worker earning 200,000 yuan a year has the time and mood to post photos of fancy afternoon tea on Xiaohongshu—he has a voice.

---
一个在县城修车厂满手黑油、一年挣5万块的学徒，他甚至没有时间完整地看完一个长视频，他的声音是真空的。
An apprentice covered in grease at a county-town auto repair shop, earning 50,000 yuan a year, doesn’t even have time to finish watching a long video—his voice is a void.

---
14亿人里，有6亿人月收入在1000元左右。这是前几年就公开的数据，到现在，这个底层逻辑没有变。
Among China’s 1.4 billion people, 600 million have a monthly income of around 1,000 yuan. This is public data from a few years ago, and the underlying reality hasn’t changed since then.

---
他们才是中国底色。
They are the true face of China.

---
他们挣不到10万，是因为他们所在的行业没有溢价。
They can’t earn 100,000 yuan a year because their industries have no premium value.

---
什么是溢价？就是你卖的东西有门槛。
What’s premium value? It means what you’re selling has entry barriers.

---
但体力没有门槛。
But physical labor has no barriers to entry.

---
当你卖的东西谁都能卖的时候，你就只能卖出咸鱼的价格。
When what you’re selling can be sold by anyone, you can only get a rock-bottom price for it.

---
我再举个例子。
Let me give you another example.

---
山东某县城的一个小学老师，工龄12年，月薪4800元，他的一年，算上绩效，也就6万出头。
A primary school teacher with 12 years of service in a county town in Shandong earns 4,800 yuan a month. Including performance bonuses, his annual income is just over 60,000 yuan.

---
他已经是那个县城的“成功人士”了，相亲市场上的一等公民。
Yet he’s considered a "success story" in that county, a top catch in the matchmaking market.

---
你问他为什么挣不到10万？
If you ask him why he can’t earn 100,000 yuan a year?

---
他会觉得你在羞辱他。
He’d think you’re insulting him.

---
因为在那个县城的物价水平和财政收入下，10万块是副县长级别的收入。
Because in that county’s price level and fiscal conditions, 100,000 yuan a year is the salary level of a deputy county magistrate.

---
你让一个普通人去挣副县长的钱，这不是逻辑问题，这是身份僭越。
Asking an ordinary person to earn the same as a deputy county magistrate isn’t a logical issue—it’s an overreach of status.

---
很多人对10万块没概念。
A lot of people have no idea what 100,000 yuan really means.

---
如果你现在手里有10万块现金，不需要多，就10万。你可以一次性拿出来，不靠贷款，不靠父母，这说明你已经超过了中国95%的同龄人。
If you currently have 100,000 yuan in cash—no more, no less—and you can take it out in one go without loans or help from your parents, that means you’re already better off than 95% of your peers in China.

---
剩下那95%的人在干什么？
What are the other 95% of people doing?

---
他们在拆东墙补西墙。
They’re robbing Peter to pay Paul.

---
10万块钱是一道透明的玻璃天花板。
100,000 yuan is a transparent glass ceiling.

---
你抬头能看见上面的灯火辉煌，但你飞不上去。
You can look up and see the bright lights above, but you can never fly high enough to reach them.

---
你撞上去，头破血流。
If you crash into it, you’ll end up bloodied and bruised.

---
你是没去看看凌晨3点的批发市场，比如武汉的白沙洲农贸市场，四季美，那里的人每一个都比你努力。
You’ve never been to a wholesale market at 3 a.m.—places like Wuhan’s Baishazhou Agricultural Market or Sijimei Market. Every single person there works harder than you do.

---
他们有的拉货拉到腰间盘突出，有的冻得手指变形。
Some of them have herniated discs from hauling goods; others have deformed fingers from working in the cold.

---
他们一年能挣到10万吗？
Can they earn 100,000 yuan a year?

---
很难。
Hardly.

---
因为他们干的活，在价值链的最末端。
Because the work they do sits at the very bottom of the value chain.

---
他们是在用命换钱，而命的价格，被市场标得很低。
They’re trading their lives for money, and the market has priced their lives very cheaply.

---
总结一下，普通中国人挣不到10万，是因为三个具体的坐标点锁死了位置：
To sum up, ordinary Chinese people can’t earn 100,000 yuan a year because three specific factors lock them in place:

---
第一，你所在的城市平均薪资基准线太低。
First, the average salary baseline of the city you live in is too low.

---
第二，你所在的行业没有任何议价能力。
Second, the industry you work in has no bargaining power whatsoever.

---
第三，你每个月的刚性成本吞噬了你的积累能力。
Third, your monthly fixed costs devour your ability to save and accumulate wealth.

---
如果你在这三个坐标点内，你就算把命搭进去，那10万块也只是一个挂在驴前面的胡萝卜。
If you’re trapped by these three factors, even if you risk your life, that 100,000 yuan will be nothing more than a carrot dangled in front of a donkey.

---
你看得到，闻得到，但你永远吃不到。
You can see it, you can smell it—but you’ll never get to taste it.

---
如果你还是不信，你可以明天去问问给你送快递的小哥。
If you still don’t believe it, go ask your delivery courier tomorrow.

---
问问他，如果他想一年净赚10万，他需要跑多少公里。
Ask him how many kilometers he’d have to cover to net 100,000 yuan a year.

---
那个数字会让你沉默。
That number will leave you speechless.

---
那个数字也会让你明白，为什么14亿人里，普通人占了大多数，而这大多数人，都在这道10万块的红线下，安静且吃力地活着。
That number will also make you understand why ordinary people make up the vast majority of China’s 1.4 billion population—and why this majority lives quietly and struggles below the 100,000-yuan threshold.
